Information about George Brown College

George Brown College, established in 1967, is a public institution located in downtown Toronto,
Ontario, Canada. Named after George Brown, a significant 19th-century politician and newspaper
publisher, the college has grown to encompass three main campuses: Casa Loma, St. James, and
Waterfront. These campuses are strategically situated to provide students with access to Toronto's
vibrant industries and cultural experiences.

The college offers over 170 full-time programs across various fields, including:
1. Arts, Design & Information Technology
2. Business
3. Community Services & Early Childhood
